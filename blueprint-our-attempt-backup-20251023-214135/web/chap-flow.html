5 Ricci Flow

The Ricci flow is a geometric evolution equation that deforms a Riemannian metric.

Definition 5.1 Ricci Flow Equation

RicciFlow.ricci_flow_equation def:riemannian_metric, def:ricci_tensor The Ricci flow equation is a parabolic PDE for a time-dependent Riemannian metric \(g(t)\):

\[ \frac{\partial g_{ij}}{\partial t} = -2 \mathrm{Ric}_{ij} \]

This equation describes how the metric evolves to become more uniform over time.

Current status: Declared as an axiom. The full definition requires setting up the theory of time-dependent metrics and their derivatives.

Theorem 5.2 Short-Time Existence

RicciFlow.short_time_existence def:ricci_flow_equation, lem:exists_pos_real For any smooth compact Riemannian manifold \((M, g_0)\), there exists \(T {\gt} 0\) and a smooth solution \(g : [0, T) \to \mathrm{Met}(M)\) to the Ricci flow equation with initial condition \(g(0) = g_0\).

Proof ▼

Current status: Declared as sorry.

The full proof requires three main steps:

  1. DeTurck’s trick: Introduce the Ricci-DeTurck flow

    \[ \frac{\partial g_{ij}}{\partial t} = -2 \mathrm{Ric}_{ij} + \mathcal{L}_W g_{ij} \]

    where \(W\) is chosen to break diffeomorphism invariance.

  2. Parabolic theory: Apply standard short-time existence theory for parabolic PDEs to the Ricci-DeTurck flow. This is the main analytic work.

  3. Pullback: Show that the Ricci-DeTurck solution can be pulled back by a family of diffeomorphisms to obtain a Ricci flow solution.
