2.1 Properties of Positive Reals

Lemma 2.1 Positive multiplication

RicciFlow.pos_mul_pos For positive real numbers \(a\) and \(b\), their product \(a \cdot b\) is positive.

Proof ▼

This follows directly from Mathlib’s mul_pos theorem.

Lemma 2.2 Square of nonzero is positive

RicciFlow.square_pos_of_ne_zero If \(x \neq 0\), then \(x^2 {\gt} 0\).

Proof ▼

This is a consequence of the fact that squares of nonzero numbers are positive, formalized using Mathlib’s sq_pos_of_ne_zero. This property is fundamental for proving positive-definiteness of the metric tensor.

Lemma 2.3 Existence of positive real

RicciFlow.exists_pos_real There exists a real number \(t\) such that \(t {\gt} 0\).

Proof ▼

Constructively, we exhibit \(t = 1\) and verify \(1 {\gt} 0\) using norm_num.

Lemma 2.4 Inverse of positive is positive

RicciFlow.inv_pos_of_pos If \(x {\gt} 0\), then \(x^{-1} {\gt} 0\).

Proof ▼

This is inv_pos.mpr from Mathlib. Used when computing scalar curvature \(R = g^{ij} \mathrm{Ric}_{ij}\) where we need the inverse metric \(g^{ij}\) to preserve positivity properties.
